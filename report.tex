\section{Introduction}
This report analyzes labour-market patterns in Armenia using Armstat Labour Force Survey microdata for 2021--2024, with 2024 analyzed separately and compared against pooled 2021--2023.

\subsection*{Research Questions}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Does sectoral employment distribution (agriculture/industry/services) differ by gender and by region?
    \item Is disability status associated with labour-force status (employed/unemployed/out of labour force)?
    \item Among employed workers, does marital status affect average monthly income?
    \item Among employed workers, do age groups differ in average paid working hours per week?
    \item Among employed workers, is unpaid domestic work associated with paid working hours?
\end{enumerate}

\section{Data Description}
Data source: Armstat Labour Force Survey files for 2021--2024. Variable harmonization and decoding are performed using the questionnaire/codebook. Two analytical datasets are created: (A) 2024 only, (B) pooled 2021--2023.

\section{Data Cleaning and Harmonization}
Cleaning decisions are recorded in \texttt{outputs/tables/cleaning\_log.csv}. The pipeline:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Harmonizes column names across years using a codebook-driven mapping.
    \item Replaces special nonresponse codes (e.g., 96/97/98/99, -9 to -5) with missing values.
    \item Applies validity rules for income and hours.
    \item Recodes labelled categories (gender, employment status, disability, marital status, sector, age groups).
\end{itemize}

\section{Methodology}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Chi-square tests of independence}: for sector vs gender/region and disability vs labour-force status.
    \item \textbf{ANOVA}: for mean income by marital status and mean paid hours by age group.
    \item \textbf{Linear regression}: paid weekly hours regressed on unpaid domestic hours (simple and adjusted models).
    \item \textbf{Confidence intervals}: visualized with 95\% CI error bars and reported where applicable.
\end{itemize}
